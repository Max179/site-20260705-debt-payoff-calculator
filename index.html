Frequently Asked Questions

How does a debt payoff calculator work?

A debt payoff calculator uses the standard amortization formula to calculate how many months it takes to pay off a debt given a fixed monthly payment and interest rate. It shows your debt-free date, total interest paid, and how extra payments can accelerate your payoff.

What is the difference between debt avalanche and snowball?

The avalanche method targets highest interest rate debt first, saving the most money mathematically. The snowball method targets smallest balance first for psychological motivation. Both work - choose what keeps you motivated.

How much extra payment should I make to pay off debt faster?

Even small extra payments make a big difference. Adding $50-$100 extra per month can shave years off your payoff timeline. Use the calculator to see exactly how much time and interest you save with different extra payment amounts.

Should I pay off debt or invest my extra money?

Pay off any debt with APR above 7-8% before investing - it's a guaranteed return. If your debt rate is lower (like mortgages), investing may yield better long-term returns. Always capture your employer 401(k) match first.

Can this calculator handle multiple debts?

This calculator handles single debt calculations. For multiple debts, calculate each one separately or use our comparison feature to see how different payment strategies impact your overall debt payoff timeline.
